CSS Positioning Examples

Static Position

The default positioning for any element.

I am a static box!

Relative Position

The box is positioned relative to its normal position.

I am a relative box!

Absolute Position

The box is positioned relative to the nearest positioned ancestor (or the viewport if none exist).

I am an absolute box!

Fixed Position

The box is positioned relative to the viewport and does not move when scrolling.

I am a fixed box!

Sticky Position

The box is positioned relative until it crosses a defined threshold (e.g., `top: 0`).

I am a sticky box! Scroll to see my effect.

Keep scrolling to see how sticky positioning works when it reaches its threshold.
